# Merops Counter-Drone System

## Overview
The **Merops** counter-drone system is an autonomous interceptor drone developed by Perennial Autonomy (formerly Project Eagle/White Stork) to intercept hostile drones, particularly Shahed-type loitering munitions.

---

## System Specifications

### Manufacturer & History
- **Manufacturer**: Perennial Autonomy
- **Former Names**: Project Eagle / White Stork
- **Founder**: Eric Schmidt (former Google CEO)
- **Timeline**: Launched as White Stork in 2023, renamed to Project Eagle Feb 2024, then Perennial Autonomy

### Interceptor Drone Details
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Model** | Surveyor |
| **Type** | Three-foot fixed-wing propeller-driven drone |
| **Speed** | Up to 175 mph (282 km/h) |
| **Cost per Shot** | $15,000 (projected below $10,000 with scale-up production) |

### Detection Sensors
- Radar and electro-optical sensors for target acquisition
- AI navigation enables autonomous operation when satellite/radio links are jammed or unavailable
- Uses thermal, radio-frequency, or radar sensors for target acquisition

---

## Operational Capabilities

### Engagement Methods
- Can ram targets OR carry small warhead
- Parachute deployment if interceptor misses target — allows reuse of the drone

### Crew Requirements
- **Crew Size**: Four-person crew (commander, pilot, two technicians)
- **Training Duration**: Just two weeks

---

## Combat Performance & Deployments

### Ukraine Operations
| Metric | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **First Deployment** | June 2024 |
| **Drones Destroyed** | Over 1,900 Russian drones by November 2025 |
| **Shahed Intercepts** | Accounted for ~40% of downed Shaheds in Ukraine (per US Army Brig. Gen. Curtis King) |

### Middle East Deployment
- **Date**: March 6, 2026
- **Location**: Iran war theater
- **Scale**: 13,000 units purchased by Pentagon in eight days

## Strategic Significance

- Cost-effective alternative to expensive interceptor missiles (Patriot costs >$3 million per shot)
- Designed specifically against Shahed-type drones and similar targets
- Part of NATO's eastern flank defense initiative under Eastern Sentry operation
